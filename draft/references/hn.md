# Hacker News

Use this reference for Show HN submissions, regular HN posts, comments, and replies.

## Audience

HN readers are technically literate, skeptical of marketing, and interested in implementation details, tradeoffs, and unexpected lessons. Write as an engineer sharing with peers.

## Show HN Structure

```text
Show HN: [Concise title]

[Opening: the problem or why this is interesting]
[Concrete implementation details, numbers, or design tradeoffs]
[Known limitations stated plainly]
[Project/repository link]
```

Keep the main body under 200 words unless the technical context genuinely requires more. HN readers scan fast.

## Requirements

- English main post.
- Chinese summary appended for the user's review, not for posting.
- State what is new or newsworthy now.
- Include at least one concrete detail: number, architecture choice, implementation constraint, or measured result.
- Acknowledge meaningful limitations. Do not manufacture a limitation for performative humility.
- Avoid calls to action such as "star us" or "join our community."

## Titles

- For launches: `Show HN: [name] – [plain description]`
- For findings: use a direct descriptive title without clickbait.
- Do not use title case mechanically. Keep proper names and canonical casing.

## Comments and Replies

- Respond to specific technical points with concrete details.
- Do not reply to every comment. Skip low-effort or adversarial comments that add nothing.
- Do not become defensive when a real limitation is identified; acknowledge it and explain the tradeoff.
- Do not necrobump old threads. Create a fresh post for a substantial new update.

## Output

1. English post ready to paste.
2. Chinese summary covering the core point, intended audience reaction, and any reception risk.
3. Comment strategy only when requested.
